About me

I'm an aspiring network, automation and systems engineer drawn to the place where infrastructure meets software. I enjoy building environments that are stable, observable and automatable, and removing repetitive work through tooling.

My journey began in theoretical science; as an avid math and physics enthusiast throughout high school, I always looked for ways to apply abstract concepts to tangible creations. This naturally led me to computer science which is a field rooted in mathematics that fundamentally transforms other industries. Over the course of my Bachelor’s degree in computer science, my focus shifted toward systems and networks, driven by a fascination with how complex systems communicate and scale. I've since applied this passion across diverse internships, from developing test suites for subsea systems and analyzing real-time architectures, to leveraging hardware features for low-latency inter-process communication.

Currently, I am in my last year of a Master's degree in computer engineering, systems and networks while wrapping up my thesis internship at NTT Data, where I focus on network automation within the context of Network as a Service (NaaS).
